# Cum & Bukkake

After sex, SexLab Survival doesn't just fade to black and move on — it leaves visible evidence on your character. Cum layers onto the player as a texture, builds up over repeated encounters, becomes something the world can see, and has to be dealt with. This page covers the **Cum** MCM page.

## Visible layering

SLS renders cum on your character through the cum-texture systems (SlaveTats and compatible frameworks — see [Mod Integrations](integrations.md)). It applies in **layers**: one encounter marks you, several mark you more heavily. The residue accumulates rather than resetting after each scene, so a busy stretch leaves your character increasingly covered until you do something about it.

## Social visibility

The layering isn't just cosmetic. Cum is **socially visible** — NPCs notice a woman who's obviously been used, and that reads into the [Misogyny & Inequality](misogyny.md) system. Walking into town covered draws exactly the kind of contempt and attention that misogyny is built to dispense. It becomes one more thing about you the world reacts to, alongside exposure and arousal.

## A need in its own right

Cum ties into arousal and can behave like one of the mod's needs — a "desperate" state that pressures you rather than sitting inert. Left unaddressed it's not neutral: it keeps you marked and keeps the world reacting. Managing it becomes part of the survival loop rather than an afterthought.

## Cleaning it off

You get it off by getting clean. Cum layers are removed by **bathing** — SLS integrates with **Bathing in Skyrim** so a proper wash strips the residue, and failing that you can clean up at **water sources** out in the world. Either way, staying presentable takes deliberate effort: find water or a bath, or wear the consequences into the next town.

For where these marks come from and the other after-effects an encounter leaves behind, see [Sex, Trauma & Effects](sex-and-effects.md).

!!! tip
    If you want the residue but not the upkeep, or the upkeep but not the social fallout, the systems are separable — tune the layering, visibility and cleaning behaviour to the balance you want.

!!! note
    All of this lives on the **Cum** MCM page. Layering, social visibility and the cleaning requirements can be tuned or disabled there.
