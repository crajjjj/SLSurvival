# Cum

This page covers the **Cum** MCM page. After a sex scene, SLS applies a body-texture overlay that accumulates in stages, can be noticed by NPCs, and has to be cleaned off. Mechanically it behaves like one of the mod's tracked states.

## Overlay stages

SLS applies the overlay through cum-texture systems (SlaveTats and compatible frameworks — see [Mod Integrations](integrations.md)). It stacks in **stages**: additional scenes raise the stage, and the overlay persists across scenes until it is cleaned, rather than clearing automatically.

## Social visibility

The overlay is flagged as **socially visible**: NPCs can react to a character carrying it, which feeds the [Misogyny & Inequality](misogyny.md) system alongside exposure and arousal. It becomes one more tracked attribute the world responds to.

## A need in its own right

Cum ties into arousal and can behave like one of the mod's needs — a "desperate" state that pressures you rather than sitting inert. Left unaddressed it's not neutral: it keeps you marked and keeps the world reacting. Managing it becomes part of the survival loop rather than an afterthought.

## Cleaning it off

You get it off by getting clean. Cum layers are removed by **bathing** — SLS integrates with **Bathing in Skyrim** so a proper wash strips the residue, and failing that you can clean up at **water sources** out in the world. Either way, staying presentable takes deliberate effort: find water or a bath, or wear the consequences into the next town.

For where these marks come from and the other after-effects an encounter leaves behind, see [Sex, Trauma & Effects](sex-and-effects.md).

!!! tip
    If you want the residue but not the upkeep, or the upkeep but not the social fallout, the systems are separable — tune the layering, visibility and cleaning behaviour to the balance you want.

!!! note
    All of this lives on the **Cum** MCM page. Layering, social visibility and the cleaning requirements can be tuned or disabled there.
